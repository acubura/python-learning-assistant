# Python Teaching Assistant Code of Conduct

## 🌟 Welcome!

Hey there! 👋 The Python Teaching Assistant community is a friendly mix of professionals, volunteers, and learners like you from all around the world. We're here to mentor, teach, connect, and grow together.

**You belong here.** Whether you're a beginner writing your first line of code or an experienced developer helping others, this space is for everyone.

## 💝 Our Simple Promise to You

We want this to be a **safe, welcoming, and encouraging** place where:
- Questions are celebrated (not judged)
- Mistakes are learning opportunities
- Kindness is the default setting

## 📋 A Few Ground Rules (Just the Essentials)

Think of these as good vibes we all agree to share:

### ✅ Be Friendly & Patient
We all start somewhere. That "simple" question might be someone's big breakthrough.

### ✅ Be Welcoming
You are welcome here regardless of your:
- Race, ethnicity, or culture
- Gender identity or expression
- Age or education level
- Background or beliefs
- Experience with Python (zero or hero — both are awesome!)

### ✅ Be Considerate
Your code, comments, and words affect real people. Pause before you post. Ask yourself: *"Would I say this to someone sitting next to me?"*

### ✅ Be Respectful
Disagreements happen! That's totally fine. But let's disagree without being disagreeable. Attack ideas, not people.

### ✅ Choose Your Words Kindly
We're all professionals here (or becoming one!). That means:
- No threats or violence (obvious, but we said it)
- No hate speech or discriminatory jokes
- No sharing personal info without permission ("doxing")
- No harassment of any kind
- If someone asks you to stop — just stop. No questions asked.

### ✅ Try to Understand, Not Just Win
We come from different places, speak different languages, and see things differently. That's our superpower! When you disagree, ask: *"What can I learn here?"*

## 🚨 Something Wrong? Let Us Know

If you see or experience anything that doesn't feel right — even if you're not sure — please **reach out**.

📧 **Email:** [acuburagamer100@gmail.com](mailto:acuburagamer100@gmail.com)

Your report will be:
- Taken seriously
- Handled privately
- Investigated fairly
- Kept confidential

You won't get in trouble for speaking up. You'll be helping make this community better for everyone.

## 🌍 Where These Rules Apply

This Code applies in all Python Teaching Assistant spaces:
- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Any chat rooms, emails, or forums we create
- Community events (online or in person)
- Even outside spaces if behavior affects our community's safety

## 💬 Questions?

If something isn't clear, or you just want to talk — **we're here for you.**

Just email us at [acuburagamer100@gmail.com](mailto:acuburagamer100@gmail.com). No question is too small.
